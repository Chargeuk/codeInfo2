# Findings

No endorsed findings.

The plan-based review, the generic engineering pass, and the generic adversarial pass did not uncover a `must_fix`, `should_fix`, or `optional_simplification` issue in the non-support-file changes for Story 54. The changed ingest code, tests, docs, and final wrapper/manual proof chain are consistent with the canonical plan, and the single scope-adjacent test timeout change in `e2e/chat-tools.spec.ts` appears justified by the shared compose-backed ingest setup cost rather than by unrelated scope creep.

## Residual Risks And Weak-Proof Areas

- `current_repository`: The "avoid repeatedly re-tokenizing the whole remaining tail" acceptance criterion is supported by direct code inspection and unit behavior proof in `server/src/ingest/chunker.ts` and `server/src/test/unit/chunker.test.ts`, but it does not have a dedicated benchmark-style instrumentation artifact. This is acceptable for the plan because the story explicitly avoids a hard performance benchmark gate.
- `current_repository`: Compatibility of persisted ingest/root metadata remains indirectly proven through unchanged route shapes, wrapper passes, and the Task 18 manual proof rather than through a dedicated before/after persisted-artifact golden file. That is acceptable for this plan because it does not introduce a storage schema migration.

## Rejected Risk Notes

### 1. `createEmbeddingDispatcher` (`current_repository`)

- Candidate risk challenged: same-turn enqueue bursts could still over-admit work or deadlock when `maxQueueSize` is tight, especially if cancellation lands while a freed slot immediately schedules the next batch.
- Decision: rejected risk.
- Direct evidence:
  - `server/src/test/unit/ingest-dispatcher.test.ts` proves the queue cap is enforced for same-turn bursts, that the fourth enqueue blocks while the queue is full, and that a freed slot refills immediately without exceeding the bound.
  - `server/src/test/unit/ingest-cancel.test.ts` proves late provider results are ignored after cancel and that `waitForIdle()` does not deadlock after production completes with queued work.
  - `server/src/test/features/ingest-embedding-dispatch.feature` and `server/src/test/features/ingest-cancel.feature` provide API-visible proof of slot filling and ignored late results.
- Proof level: direct.
- What remains weak: no extra producer/consumer proof was added for a combined `maxQueueSize=0` plus cancel plus multi-batch interleaving in the full ingest path, but the unit and Cucumber coverage is strong enough that this does not rise to a finding.

### 2. `chunkText` / `buildProseChunks` (`current_repository`)

- Candidate risk challenged: the large-text prose path could still fall back to repeated whole-tail retokenization or violate token bounds on a very large Markdown file with headings, lists, and fenced blocks.
- Decision: rejected risk with residual weak-proof note.
- Direct evidence:
  - `server/src/test/unit/chunker.test.ts` proves large prose files take the prose route, large non-prose files stay on the generic route, and the prose route still enforces token limits while removing blank chunks.
  - `e2e/ingest.spec.ts` and the Task 18 manual proof record `DEV-0000054:large_text_path_selected` on the mounted large planning fixture.
  - `server/src/ingest/chunker.ts` uses `findLocalBoundaryCut(...)` and `sliceToFitLocally(...)` specifically to favor local cuts before broader fallback slicing.
- Proof level: mixed direct/indirect.
- What remains weak: there is still no benchmark-style token-count instrumentation proving the exact reduction in whole-tail retokenization under worst-case prose input. That gap is acceptable under this plan because Story 54 explicitly rejects a benchmark harness or hard SLA.

### 3. `processRun` / `publishTerminalStatus` (`current_repository`)

- Candidate risk challenged: a deletions-only AST-relevant re-embed finishing with `fileTotal=0` could still race with late cancel/finalization work and either publish `completed` incorrectly or reintroduce a non-numeric terminal `percent`.
- Decision: rejected risk.
- Direct evidence:
  - `server/src/test/unit/ingest-reembed.test.ts` directly proves deletions-only delta re-embed finishes `completed` with `percent === 0`.
  - `server/src/test/unit/ingest-cancel.test.ts` directly proves late cancel on delta no-op and deletions-only re-embed fast paths does not publish `completed` or `skipped`.
  - Task 18 manual proof recorded a follow-up deletions-only re-embed whose `/ingest/status/:runId` payload returned `fileTotal: 0` and `percent: 0`.
- Proof level: direct.
- What remains weak: the zero-file terminal-percent proof and the late-cancel fast-path proof are covered in separate tests rather than one single combined test case, but together they cover the contradictory state strongly enough that this does not rise to a finding.

## Additional Notes

- `e2e/chat-tools.spec.ts` was reviewed as the one scope-adjacent non-support-file change. The timeout increase is localized, the comment accurately describes why the test now pays the same compose-backed ingest setup cost as `e2e/ingest.spec.ts`, and the unchanged `playwright.config.ts` still keeps the suite serialized with `workers: 1`. That change is therefore not endorsed as a scope-creep finding.
