- Check shared-state surfaces touched by the queue change: Mongo queue records, cleanup-blocked retry timers, event listeners, temp paths, screenshot output, and any lock or singleton resources.
- Compare reader and writer pairs for persisted queue data to ensure writes are atomic enough for readers, startup recovery, and delete-retry paths.
- Inspect lifecycle transitions end to end: enqueue, waiting, running, terminal completion, cleanup-blocked stall, retry, restart recovery, and eventual deletion.
- Verify tests that mutate shared state remain parallel-safe and do not rely on cross-suite serialization accidentally provided by a specific runner configuration.
- Challenge malformed, missing, contradictory, or partially written queue state, especially mixed-shape rows left by restart or cleanup failures.
- Re-check any rename, ignore-rule, suffix, symlink, or permission changes in wrappers and BATS vendor files for silent exclusion from default validation paths.
